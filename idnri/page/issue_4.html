Automotive

Back to previous page

Summary

Earlier 2019, Industry Minister Airlangga Hartarto says several investors are committed to investing US$900 million in the automotive industry. Investors in the automotive industry are confident in Indonesia, particularly because Indonesia is a competent producer of chemicals, glasses and tires needed by the industry. The minister stressed the importance of investment in manufacturing industries, including in the automotive sector, because such industries employed a large number of workers and their products could reduce Indonesia’s dependency on exports. Automotive is a growing industry in Indonesia as the government also aimed to export 100,000 vehicles in 2019.
Image : indonesianmotorshow.com

Market Divergence

In terms of market size, Indonesia is the largest car market in Southeast Asia and the ASEAN region, controlling about one third of total annual car sales in ASEAN, followed by Thailand in the second position. Indonesia not only has a large population (258 million), but is also characterized by having a rapidly growing middle class. Together, these two factors create strong consumer strength in the automotive industry. The government is determined to turn Indonesia into a global production center for car manufacturing and wants to see large car manufacturers set up factories in Indonesia. Indonesia is determined to replace Thailand as the largest car production center in Southeast Asia and the ASEAN region. In the long run, the government wants to turn Indonesia into an independent car manufacturing country that manufactures car units with all components manufactured in Indonesia.

Automotive Technology Progression

The Indonesian government has been keen to encourage local manufacturing in the automotive industry and introduced protective measures to encourage technology transfer. Based on an expert from Indonesian Automotive Industry Association (Gaikindo), there are five key points that determine the competitiveness of a nation's automotive manufacturing industry: (1) infrastructure, (2) technology, (3) costs and productivity, (4) human resources, and (5) the electricity supply. Starting from 2011, Industry 4.0 was introduced which IT based system / digitalization (including electrification) was being implemented in automotive manufacturing in Indonesia.

Emissions and Pollution

In the current dry season, Jakarta has consistently ranked among the world’s most polluted cities, based on data from Air Visual, a Swiss-based group that monitors air quality. In 2016, the municipal government ordered curbs on private cars governed by whether their license-plate numbers were odd or even, to reduce traffic jams on main thoroughfares. Based on the World Resources Institute Indonesia, a strategic plan to cut pollution based on an inventory of emissions would be a better solution. The rules of odd and even license-plate will only make the citizens buy more cars or change their license number.
